## Getting Started

### What is a Call for Papers (CFP)?

A Call for Papers is the process conferences use to collect talk proposals from potential speakers. You submit your talk ideas, organizers review them, and if selected, you'll be invited to present at the conference.

### What You'll Need

- **Account**: Create an account using email/password or social login
- **Talk Proposal**: Title, summary, and optional detailed description
- **Slides** (optional): Can be uploaded during submission or later
- **Response Time**: Be prepared to respond to acceptance within the deadline

### System Features for Speakers

- Submit unlimited talk proposals
- Track submission status in real-time
- Upload or update slides anytime
- Accept or decline talk acceptances
- View the published conference schedule

## Creating an Account

### Sign Up Options

You can create an account in two ways:

#### Option 1: Username and Password

1. Click **Sign Up** in the navigation menu
2. Enter your information:
   - Username (will be your login name)
   - Email address (for notifications)
   - Full name (displayed with your talks)
   - Password (minimum 8 characters)
3. Click **Create Account**
4. You'll be automatically logged in

#### Option 2: Social Login

Click one of the social login buttons:

- **Sign in with Google**
- **Sign in with GitHub**
- **Sign in with Apple**
- **Sign in with Facebook**
- **Sign in with LinkedIn**

Benefits:
- No password to remember
- Faster sign-up process
- Uses your existing verified email

Note: Social login availability depends on conference configuration.

### Logging In

1. Click **Login** in the navigation menu
2. Enter your username/email and password
3. Or click your social login provider
4. You'll be redirected to your dashboard

## Submitting a Talk

### Before You Submit

**Review the CFP Requirements:**

- Submission deadline (no late submissions accepted)
- Talk length/format requirements
- Topic guidelines or themes
- Any special requirements (slides format, etc.)

**Prepare Your Proposal:**

- Write a compelling title (attention-grabbing but descriptive)
- Draft a short summary (typically 2-3 sentences)
- Optionally write a detailed description
- Prepare slides if available (can be added later)

### The Submission Process

1. **Navigate to Submit Talk**
   - Click **Submit Talk** in the navigation menu
   - Or go to `/talks/submit`

2. **Fill in Required Fields**

   **Title** (Required)
   - Clear, descriptive title for your talk
   - Should convey the topic and value
   - Examples:
     - "Introduction to Rust for Systems Programming"
     - "Building Scalable APIs with GraphQL"
     - "Security Best Practices for Cloud Deployments"

   **Short Summary** (Required)
   - Brief overview of your talk (2-3 sentences)
   - What will attendees learn?
   - Who is the target audience?
   - Example: "Learn how to build scalable REST APIs using Rust and Axum. This talk covers routing, middleware, database integration, and deployment strategies. Suitable for developers with basic Rust knowledge."

   **Long Description** (Optional)
   - Detailed outline of your talk content
   - Key topics you'll cover
   - Prerequisites or assumed knowledge
   - Learning outcomes
   - Use this space to convince organizers your talk is valuable

3. **Add Labels/Tags** (Optional)

   Select relevant labels to categorize your talk:
   - Topics: Security, DevOps, Cloud, AI/ML, Web Development, etc.
   - Audience Level: Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced
   - Format: Tutorial, Case Study, Deep Dive, etc.

   Labels help organizers categorize submissions and help attendees find relevant talks.

4. **Upload Slides** (Optional)

   You can upload slides now or add them later:
   - Click **Choose File** or **Browse**
   - Select your slide file (PDF, PPTX, etc.)
   - Supported formats shown on the form
   - Maximum file size shown on the form

   Note: You can always return to upload or update slides later.

5. **Review and Submit**

   - Review all information for accuracy
   - Check spelling and grammar
   - Ensure your summary is clear and compelling
   - Click **Submit Talk**

6. **Confirmation**

   - You'll see a success message
   - Your talk will appear in "My Talks"
   - You may receive an email confirmation (if configured)

### After Submission

- You can edit your submission until the deadline
- Upload or update slides anytime
- Track your submission status on the dashboard
- Wait for organizers to review (this may take time)

## Managing Your Submissions

### Viewing Your Talks

Navigate to **My Talks** (`/talks/mine`) to see all your submissions.

### Talk Information Displayed

For each talk you'll see:

- **Title**: Your talk title
- **Status**: Current state (submitted, pending, accepted, rejected)
- **Submission Date**: When you submitted
- **Labels**: Tags applied to your talk
- **Actions**: View details, edit, upload slides

### Editing a Submission

Before the CFP deadline, you can edit your talks:

1. Go to **My Talks**
2. Click on a talk to view details
3. Click **Edit**
4. Update any fields
5. Click **Save Changes**

Note: After the deadline, editing may be disabled.

### Deleting a Submission

If you need to withdraw a talk:

1. Go to **My Talks**
2. Click on the talk
3. Click **Delete**
4. Confirm deletion

Warning: This cannot be undone. If your talk was accepted, deleting it may cause scheduling issues.

## Understanding Talk Status

Your submission moves through different states:

### Submitted

**What it means**: Your talk has been submitted and is awaiting review.

**What happens next**:
- Organizers will review your submission
- They may rate and discuss it with other organizers
- They may add labels to categorize it

**What you should do**:
- Wait for organizers to complete their review
- Check back periodically for status updates
- Ensure your contact email is correct

**Timeline**: This phase can last from days to weeks depending on the conference.

### Pending

**What it means**: Congratulations! Organizers have accepted your talk and are waiting for your confirmation.

**What happens next**:
- You'll receive an email notification (if configured)
- You need to respond: accept or decline
- There's usually a deadline to respond

**What you should do**:
- **Accept** if you can commit to presenting
- **Decline** if you cannot present (travel, schedule, etc.)
- Respond quickly - organizers need to finalize the schedule
- Only accept if you're confident you can attend

**Important**: Accepting means you're committing to present. Don't accept unless you're sure.

### Accepted

**What it means**: You've confirmed you'll present, and your talk is officially in the conference lineup!

**What happens next**:
- Organizers will assign you a time slot and room
- You'll see your talk on the public schedule
- You may receive additional communications about logistics

**What you should do**:
- Prepare your presentation
- Upload final slides before the deadline
- Note your scheduled time and room
- Confirm any AV or technical requirements
- Book travel and accommodations

### Rejected

**What it means**: Unfortunately, your talk wasn't selected for this conference.

**Why this happens**:
- High volume of quality submissions
- Talk doesn't fit conference themes
- Similar topics already selected
- Time/space constraints

**What you should do**:
- Don't take it personally - rejection is common
- Consider the feedback if provided
- Submit to other conferences
- Try again next year with an improved proposal

## Responding to Acceptances

### When Your Talk is Accepted

If organizers accept your talk, the status changes to **Pending** and you'll need to respond.

### How to Respond

1. Go to **My Talks**
2. Find the talk with **Pending** status
3. Click to view details
4. You'll see two buttons:
   - **Accept**: Confirm you'll present
   - **Decline**: Indicate you cannot present

### Accepting a Talk

**Before you click Accept:**

- ✓ Can you attend the conference on the scheduled dates?
- ✓ Can you cover travel costs (unless conference provides support)?
- ✓ Can you commit time to prepare the presentation?
- ✓ Do you have required equipment/internet for virtual presentations?
- ✓ Can you meet the slides deadline?

**After clicking Accept:**

- Status changes to **Accepted**
- You're committed to presenting
- Organizers will schedule your talk
- You'll receive further instructions
- Backing out now affects the conference schedule

### Declining a Talk

It's better to decline than to accept and cancel later.

**Valid reasons to decline:**
- Schedule conflicts
- Travel not possible
- Budget constraints
- Personal or professional commitments
- Health issues

**To decline:**

1. Click **Decline**
2. Optionally provide a reason (helps organizers)
3. Status changes to **Rejected**
4. Your spot opens for other speakers

Note: Declining professionally maintains a good relationship for future submissions.

### Response Deadlines

- Organizers typically set a deadline (e.g., 2 weeks)
- Respond as quickly as possible
- If you miss the deadline, organizers may revoke acceptance
- Check your email for deadline reminders

## Uploading Slides

### When to Upload Slides

You can upload slides:

- **During submission**: If you already have slides prepared
- **After acceptance**: Most common approach
- **Before the deadline**: Conference will specify a deadline
- **Anytime**: Update slides as many times as needed

### How to Upload Slides

#### Method 1: During Submission

1. When submitting a talk, use the file upload field
2. Click **Choose File** or **Browse**
3. Select your slide file
4. Continue with submission

#### Method 2: After Submission

1. Go to **My Talks**
2. Click on your talk
3. Click **Upload Slides** or **Update Slides**
4. Choose your file
5. Click **Upload**
6. Confirmation message appears

### Slide Requirements

Check the conference requirements for:

- **File formats**: PDF, PPTX, KEY, etc.
- **File size limit**: Usually 10-50 MB
- **Deadline**: When slides must be uploaded
- **Content guidelines**: Any specific requirements

### Slide Best Practices

**Format:**
- PDF is most compatible across systems
- Include your name and contact info on first slide
- Add slide numbers for reference during Q&A

**Content:**
- Use readable fonts (minimum 24pt for body text)
- High contrast between text and background
- Avoid walls of text - use bullet points
- Include code samples if relevant (large font)
- Credit sources and images

**Technical:**
- Test slides on conference equipment if possible
- Bring backup on USB drive
- Have web link to slides as backup
- Export animations to ensure compatibility

### Updating Slides

You can update slides multiple times:

1. Go to **My Talks**
2. Click on your talk
3. Click **Update Slides**
4. Upload new version
5. Old version is replaced

**When to update:**
- Fix errors or typos
- Add new information
- Incorporate feedback
- Update after dry run

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Submission Questions

**Q: How many talks can I submit?**

A: You can submit as many talks as you'd like. However, quality over quantity is recommended. Focus on your best ideas.

**Q: Can I submit the same talk to multiple conferences?**

A: Yes, it's common to submit talks to multiple conferences. Just ensure you can fulfill commitments if multiple conferences accept you.

**Q: Can I edit my submission after submitting?**

A: Yes, you can edit your talk until the CFP deadline. After the deadline, editing may be disabled.

**Q: What makes a good talk proposal?**

A: Clear title, compelling summary, realistic scope, clear learning outcomes, and demonstration of expertise.

**Q: Should I submit talks on topics I'm still learning?**

A: It's best to speak on topics where you have solid experience. Teaching is different from learning.

**Q: Do I need slides to submit?**

A: No, slides are optional at submission. You can upload them later, but there will be a deadline before the conference.

### Status Questions

**Q: How long does review take?**

A: This varies by conference. Some review quickly (days), others take weeks. Check the conference timeline.

**Q: Can I ask about my submission status?**

A: Yes, but be patient. Organizers are reviewing many submissions. Only reach out if the stated review period has passed.

**Q: What if my status never changes from "Submitted"?**

A: This likely means it wasn't selected. Some conferences only notify accepted speakers. Check the conference policy.

**Q: If my talk is rejected, will I get feedback?**

A: This depends on the conference. Many don't provide feedback due to volume, but you can politely request it.

### Acceptance Questions

**Q: What if I accept but later can't present?**

A: Contact organizers immediately. Last-minute cancellations are very disruptive. They may find a replacement or adjust the schedule.

**Q: Can I suggest a co-presenter?**

A: Contact organizers about adding a co-presenter. Some systems support this, others may need manual updates.

**Q: Will the conference cover my expenses?**

A: This varies widely. Some conferences provide:
- Travel reimbursement
- Hotel accommodation
- Conference pass
- Honorarium

Check the conference speaker benefits or ask organizers.

**Q: Can I present remotely?**

A: Depends on the conference policy. Some allow virtual presentations, others require in-person. Check conference guidelines.

**Q: What if I have scheduling conflicts?**

A: Contact organizers as soon as possible. They may be able to adjust your time slot if notified early enough.

### Technical Questions

**Q: I forgot my password. How do I reset it?**

A: Currently, contact the system administrator. Password reset functionality may be added in future versions.

**Q: Can I change my email address?**

A: Currently, contact the system administrator to update your email address.

**Q: My slide upload is failing. What should I do?**

A: Check file size limit and format requirements. Try converting to PDF. If issues persist, contact organizers.

**Q: Can I use social login if I created a password account?**

A: These are typically separate. You may be able to link accounts by contacting the administrator.

## Tips for Success

### Writing Great Proposals

**Title Tips:**
- Be specific: "Container Orchestration with Kubernetes" not "DevOps Talk"
- Indicate value: "Building High-Performance APIs" not "APIs Overview"
- Target audience: "Rust for Python Developers" not "Intro to Rust"
- Avoid jargon unless it's a technical audience

**Summary Tips:**
- First sentence: What is this talk about?
- Second sentence: What will attendees learn?
- Third sentence: Who should attend?
- Keep it concise but informative
- Use active voice
- Avoid marketing buzzwords

**Description Tips:**
- Outline the talk structure
- List 3-5 key takeaways
- Mention any demos or code examples
- State prerequisites clearly
- Show your expertise subtly

### Increasing Acceptance Chances

1. **Submit early**: Shows enthusiasm and gives more time for review
2. **Follow guidelines**: Respect length limits and format requirements
3. **Be specific**: Vague proposals are easy to reject
4. **Show value**: What will attendees gain?
5. **Demonstrate expertise**: Reference experience, projects, or credentials
6. **Proofread**: Typos and errors suggest lack of care
7. **Be realistic**: Don't promise more than you can deliver
8. **Read past talks**: Look at previous conference schedules for inspiration
9. **Get feedback**: Have colleagues review before submitting
10. **Be authentic**: Speak on what you genuinely know and care about

### Preparing Your Talk

**After acceptance:**

1. **Start early**: Don't wait until the last minute
2. **Outline first**: Structure before slides
3. **Tell a story**: Beginning, middle, end with clear narrative
4. **Practice multiple times**: Time yourself, refine content
5. **Get feedback**: Present to colleagues or friends
6. **Prepare for Q&A**: Anticipate questions
7. **Have backups**: Multiple copies of slides, backup laptop
8. **Check equipment**: Test with conference setup if possible
9. **Plan timing**: Know what to cut if running long
10. **Stay calm**: Remember, the audience wants you to succeed

### During Your Talk

- Arrive early to test equipment
- Introduce yourself and the topic clearly
- Speak clearly and at a moderate pace
- Make eye contact with audience
- Use live demos sparingly (they often fail)
- Engage with questions respectfully
- Stay on time (watch the clock)
- Summarize key points at the end
- Provide resources for further learning
- Thank the organizers and audience

## Troubleshooting

### Login Issues

**Problem**: Can't log in with username/password.

**Solutions**:
- Verify username and password are correct
- Check caps lock is off
- Try password reset (contact admin if needed)
- Try social login if you signed up that way
- Clear browser cache and cookies
- Try a different browser

**Problem**: Social login not working.

**Solutions**:
- Ensure social provider is configured (check with conference)
- Try a different social provider
- Create a password account as alternative
- Check you're using the correct social account
- Disable ad blockers that might block OAuth

### Submission Issues

**Problem**: Can't submit talk - button doesn't work.

**Solutions**:
- Check all required fields are filled
- Verify title and summary meet length requirements
- Try refreshing the page
- Try a different browser
- Check browser console for errors (F12 → Console)
- Contact organizers if issue persists

**Problem**: Submission deadline passed.

**Solutions**:
- Check if there's an extension (sometimes announced)
- Contact organizers to request late submission consideration
- Plan to submit to a different conference
- Mark your calendar for next year's CFP

### Upload Issues

**Problem**: Slide upload fails.

**Solutions**:
- Check file size is under the limit
- Verify file format is supported (PDF recommended)
- Try compressing the file
- Convert to PDF if using PowerPoint
- Check your internet connection
- Try a different browser
- Contact organizers to send slides via email as backup

**Problem**: Uploaded wrong file.

**Solutions**:
- Use "Update Slides" to replace with correct file
- Contact organizers if you can't update
- No harm done - you can update as many times as needed

### Status Issues

**Problem**: Status hasn't changed in weeks.

**Solutions**:
- Check conference timeline - review may still be ongoing
- Look for announcement emails about delays
- Contact organizers politely for status update
- Be patient - reviewing takes time with many submissions

**Problem**: Accidentally declined an acceptance.

**Solutions**:
- Contact organizers immediately
- Explain the mistake
- They may be able to revert it if caught quickly
- No guarantees - be careful when clicking buttons

### Display Issues

**Problem**: Page not loading or looks broken.

**Solutions**:
- Refresh the page (Ctrl+F5 or Cmd+Shift+R)
- Clear browser cache and cookies
- Try a different browser
- Disable browser extensions temporarily
- Check your internet connection
- Try on a different device

**Problem**: Can't see "My Talks" page.

**Solutions**:
- Ensure you're logged in
- Check URL is correct (/talks/mine)
- Try logging out and back in
- Verify your account has speaker permissions
- Contact administrator if issue persists

### Getting Help

If you encounter issues not covered here:

1. **Check your email**: Look for communications from organizers
2. **Review conference website**: May have additional FAQs
3. **Contact organizers**: Email or contact form
4. **Provide details**: Include browser, screenshots, error messages
5. **Be patient**: Organizers may be volunteers with day jobs
